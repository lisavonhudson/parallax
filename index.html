The Seven Building Blocks of a Successful Private Exchange

Healthcare Consumers

that have benefited from years of employer-sponsored health coverage are realizing a low healthcare acumen and limited understanding of the more common elements of industry. Althought the notion of premiums, co-pays, deductibles, and co-insurance have become second nature to many healthcare consumers, the average consumer is in need of a more in-depth education.

Healthcare & Private Exchange

marketplace continue to evolve and develop, and tomorrow's healthcare consumer - those consumers that encompass the younger demographic/generation - will likely be exposed to a very different experience. the advent of consumerism within the healthcare industry will deliver a healthcare experience previous generatons have never experienced before.

While there are disparate offerings in the Marketplace that are not easily comparable and often times very different form one another, the term "exchange", in general, is now used with such frequency that it causes an

Abundance of Confusion.

An important first step when evaluating any type of private health insurance exchange is to determine if it effectively offers all of the seven essental building blocks.

Credibility

is a building block that must be taken seriously. When considering the rollout of healthcare.gov, and the amount of press that it has since received, the idea of credibility has become more importnat than ever within the healthcare exchange business. The shareholders must believe the exchange is going to work and be successful, and avoid engaging with vendors whose marketing materials may ultimately outpace its actual resume.

Potential exchange providers should always be measured from a business perspective:

Evidence of past successes
Revenue metrics
Breadth of client base
Number of enrollments and members using the technology today

Return on investment

A private exchange shouldn't be cost prohibitive and should provide an easy-to-see Return on Investment model for all stakeholders involved.

The first stakeholder

is the beneficiary, or end user, of the online benefit shopping experience. With choice and information being two elements that need to be served by the private exchange, employees must see incremental value with the benefit selections they are making.

Value enhancements most commonly include:

The ability to lower total out-of-pocket medical costs
Tax savings through vehicles like health savings accounts
The opportunity to add previously unaffordable ancillary benefits to the famiy's portfolio

Second Stakeholder

today's employer

will associate their return on investment with cost and predicability. With the continued escalation of healthcare costes, predictability has become more important than ever before. Employers must be able to migrate their employees to an exchange without paying more in administrative fees when compared to the potential savings realized throught defined contribution, hight deductible plan, and the "buy down" effect that typically results when consumers make descisions using their own dollars.

Third Stakeholder

The third, and final, stakeholder is the health plan, benefit consultant, or agent that will be selling the private exchange services. Realizing revenue or staying revenue neutral is this group's primary return on investment. However, beneficiary satisfaction - the promise of the private exchange - is just as important as adding new products as a means of growing their revenue base. A positive user experience has the potential to develop into long-term client success and new commission streams.

What's most important?

Staying revenue neutral?
Beneficiary satisfaction?
New products?
(Tricked ya! The answer is "all of the above.")

Meaningful product choice

Although the term 'meaningful' may represent different things to different people, meaningful choice and flexibility for the broker starts with the availability of medical, voluntary, and ancillary products. As medical commissions decrease, brokers are seeing enormous value in discovering additional revenue sources while delivering meaningful choice to their exchange participants. As individual consumers further thier medical decision-making authority, they are realizing the value of voluntary and ancillary supplement choices that effect other aspects of their life beyond health insurance.

Paradox of choice

speaks to the contradictory position that too much choice can potentially be problematic. Consequently, it is critically important to be conscious about the amount of Meaningful Product Choice that is provided to private exchange participants.

Post enrollment capabilities & services

are just as essential as teh intital selection of the plans and benefits off the shelf. By establishing an environment that the customer repeatedly visits - not because they have to, but because they want to - and altering that realationship to no loinger be just a transaction, becomes a very powerful idea.

It is important to have the

Flexibility

to be able to turn on or off available functionalities, while allowing for the individual components to be assembled as needed:

  • Benefits counsellors
  • Checking on balances from a healthcare account
  • Wellness capabilities
  • Transparency tools

Serving all Beneficiaries

An exchange needs to Serve all Beneficiaries tht the exchange sponsor seeks to serve. While obvious on the surface, a cohesive experience that addresses all the membes of a family, regardless of subsidies, becomes an ever present and all important factor.

Solutions

should be made available to the four primary demographic populations that are most common with an employer: full-time active, part-time/seasonal, early retiree, and Medicare retiree. If your book of business has a diverse client mix that encompasses a variety of demographics, consider choosing to work with a private exchange that is able to harness all segments withing the employee-retiree spectrum.

Requirements of great software

Being able to answer questions before they are asked is one of the Requirements of a Great software Experience.

The technology thatpwers the exchange platform needs to perform several functions. It must be fully

Functional & intuitive

to users - specifically those who may be shopping online for the firest time - deployed quickly, and configurable for a variety of client needs. The software implementation process should focus on delivering design philosophies that go beyond preferred functionality and that become mandatory for the private exchange business to thrive.

Decision Support Tools

should be designed to help employees make the best possible health benefit enrollment decisions for themselves and their dependents by optimizing employer contributuions, their own financials, and federally available subsidies. It is imperative that an intuitive pathway be laid out for employees to navigate through a variety of plans, ancillary products, and funding scenarios.

Decision support

should also utilize transparency tools - tools that enable employees to see the real cost of pharmacy and healthcare services as they maximize their health care dollars in thigh deductible environments. furthermore, this same technology should help the brokers, benefit consultants, and employers make better strategic decisions.

Intuitive and robust decision support tools can

Help employers understand the basics of the Affordable Care Act
Detail the imparct of the beneficiary's in-and-out-of network decisions and choices, while narrowing the network footprint
Employ the beneficiary - the consumer willing to answer the decision support questions - as an important design element
Manage 'what if' scenarios by providing decision support around both critical and meaningful health and medical possibilities (having a baby, needing major/monor surgery, etc.)

Mega Trends

While the development of the private exchange is still in its infancy, there are a number of Megatrends that continue to shape and form the future of this particular healthcare landscape.

With an estimated 40 Million private exchange enrollees forecasted by 2018, exchanges will evolve on pace with the evolution of

Consumer healthcare literacy

As a result, it will be critical for private exchanges to appropriately adapt its technology. Although today's level of consumer healthcare education is relatively low, current technology needs to accurately service that level while adjusting accordingly over time.

All things being equal, consumers will continue to do business with the people and brands they know, like, and trust. As might be expected then,

Trust is the key

to doing business, regardless of what may or may not happen with megatrends. Whether private exchanges are the avenue for consumerism or not, people will continue to become more participative in their health care decision making.
